Piste centralizes the data though, so a family can register for multiple clubs and have communications in a single place. This was a big deal for me because I used to train between 4 different clubs locally. It's rough maintaining schedules and finding opportunities to get more open fencing time when none of the club data is connected. Since membership data and billing options are centralized, registering for open fencing at another club can be done in a few clicks. There's network effects here, where the more clubs in your region onboarded should see higher engagement across clubs, and less managing of customer data since it's maintained by the member.\n\nI think the primary additions are more options around how membership billing and how classes and private lessons get scheduled. Especially in regards to cancellations/rescheduling of private lessons with automated notifications. ScheduleFencing is a little more rigid with fewer options. Piste will also have a generous free tier for smaller clubs. When you look at a breakdown of clubs in the US the majority are actually very small and probably wouldn't make sense to pay for a class management tool. Offering a free tier aimed at supporting the ~50% of clubs in the US that are categorized as \"small\" I think would make a big difference to the community.\n\nI'd also like to introduce a skill leveling system that allows you to essentially track skill level of students and \"promote\" them to higher leveled classes. This essentially becomes a permissioning system for enrollments with the option for coaches to give manual approval to override course requirements. Or one-off clinics.\n\nIn addition, there will be direct integration with our equipment store (which I'm building out now).
